## 1. The gap this fixes

The current case study starts in 2021 with Arthur's critique of "the first prototype." But the first prototype wasn't 2021 — it was 2018, and it has a rich, well-documented design history: three public deployments at CalArts, written performer feedback, a NIME paper, and a visual record running from a green-screen graph demo to the pastel interface that lasted years. The existing page already contains two HTML comments (lines 31–69) holding origin-story notes that were written for exactly this purpose:

- **"NOTE FOR FUTURE REVISION"**: collapsing the daylight between exploring and capturing — two modes of one interface.
- **"ORIGIN STORY"**: Tymoczko's papers read as design specs ("I can build this car... what happens if I give other people the keys?"); EXPLORE came first (too many dimensions to hold in your head); SHARE from the CalArts milieu (Kapur, Cook, Wang, Salazar, laptop orchestra); CAPTURE suggested by Arthur years later "like Sudoku."

The prologue should make the Arthur beat land harder: by the time he said "you lose what you had," the tool already had three years of history as an explore/share instrument, and the capture gap was its known blind spot.

## 4. Design-rationale snippets worth adapting (thesis-rewrite Ch. 3)

These read like case-study prose already; adapt rather than quote:

- **Shape encodes neighbor count**: hexagon = diatonic (6 neighbors), rectangle = acoustic (4), leaning parallelograms = harmonic major/minor. "Shape is therefore not decoration but a legend the user can learn by counting."
- **Color encodes root**, hues mapped around the circle of fifths (Scriabin's mapping): "a wash of similar hues on screen means a neighborhood of closely related keys."
- **Direct manipulation framing**: "There is no command to learn and no blank page to face; the interface's first question is not 'what do you want to write?' but 'what if you go here?'"
- **The smoothness slider**: "a knob on the tension between smoothness and surprise."
- **Scalar supersets**: "the interface's escape hatch from its own locality" (pivot-chord jumps to distant scales).
- **Why 48 nodes first**: uniform cardinality (melody mapping by scale-degree substitution), notational legibility (modulation = change of accidentals, not note count), interface density (fits a first-timer's working memory). The cost: no whole-tone — "that favorite of Debussy's."
- **The asymmetry defense**: scale level is a neutral map ("everywhere the music could go"); chord level is a voice with a personality (Boyd's jazz voicings).
- **NIME 2019, Porto Alegre**: first public academic appearance; lesson: "a live demonstration communicates a navigable pitch space better than any static description of one."
